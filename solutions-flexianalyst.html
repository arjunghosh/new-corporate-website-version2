§ 01WHAT IT FEELS LIKE

Two questions, in your conference room.

CHIEF REVENUE OFFICER
"Why did West region miss Q2 by 12%?"
FLEXIANALYST
West missed because two enterprise renewals slipped to Q3 — Ascendia (₹4.2cr, signed 11 Jul) and Northwind (₹3.1cr, in legal review). Pipeline coverage entering Q2 was 2.4× — below the 3.0× minimum we agreed on for that region in May. Two contributing factors: the field-marketing cuts in March hit West first, and the new SE we hired covered for the parental-leave gap but only ramped from week 6.

Confidence: high. Sources: SFDC, finance-actuals, headcount-roster, KPI-registry. Caveats: I don't see industry-vertical data for one segment.
CRO
"What should I tell the board?"
FLEXIANALYST
I'd frame it three ways: (1) two known slips, both in renewal — not new business risk; (2) structural pipeline shortfall in West that we under-reacted to in April — that's the lesson; (3) Q3 path back — Ascendia plus eight in-quarter deals already at proposal stage gets us to 104% of plan. I can pull the supporting Power BI page if you want to walk through it before tomorrow.
§ 02WHY IT'S DIFFERENT

Three things generic AI analysts miss.

DIFF · 01

Sits on FlexiContext

Uses your KPI registry, your hierarchies, your exceptions. "Pipeline coverage" means what your sales-ops team agreed it means — not whatever the model guessed.

DIFF · 02

Cited, not confident

Every claim points back to the source: which table, which row, which definition. If it's not grounded, it says so. No confident hallucination on the board call.

DIFF · 03

Deployable in your tenancy

Runs inside your Microsoft / Databricks tenancy. Your data residency, your IAM, your audit trail. Nothing crosses the perimeter.
